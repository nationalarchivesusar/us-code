# Public Law 3-25 — CRACK Act

## Disposition
No executable catch-up plan. The later repeal chain prevents a safe wholesale insertion, while part of the executed-amendment question remains legally ambiguous.

## Review
The CRACK Act contained a large mix of standalone national-security rules and direct Title 18 amendments. Public Law 38-263, § 5, repealed Titles I through IV and replaced the national-security framework. Public Law 38-264, § 5(a), expressly repealed Titles V and VI **and any amendments made by those titles**, replacing the organized-crime and racketeering framework.

The Title V–VI result is clear: their Code amendments should not now be inserted. For Titles I–IV, Public Law 38-263 repeals the titles but does not use Public Law 38-264’s more explicit “and any amendments made” language. That creates a genuine question whether already-executed amendments were intended to remain while the parent framework was repealed. The current repository never incorporated those early CRACK amendments, and inserting the entire repealed framework now would contradict the replacement legislation.

## Current treatment
No broad CRACK plan is approved. A provision-by-provision net-effect project could later identify any discrete Title I–IV amendment that clearly survived and remains consistent with Public Law 38-263. The present package does not guess.
