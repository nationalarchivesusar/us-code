# Public Law 38-266 — Speedy Trial Act of 2026

## Disposition
Approved for BAT application after automatic current-hash generation and scratch testing.

## Enacted effect
Section 2(b) repeals 18 U.S.C. chapter 208 in its entirety. Sections 3 through 5 enact replacement time limits, exclusions, sanctions, and litigation deadlines. Section 6 amends Public Law 4-32 rather than a Code section.

## Section-number resolution
The replacement mapping is:

- Act § 3 → 18 U.S.C. § 3161, **Time limits and exclusions**;
- Act § 4 → 18 U.S.C. § 3162, **Sanctions**;
- Act § 5 → 18 U.S.C. § 3163, **Deadlines**.

This is not arbitrary. Section 4(b) expressly requires conduct to be consistent with “section 3161 of this chapter,” compelling placement of section 3 at § 3161. The next two enacted subjects track the established chapter sequence: sanctions at § 3162 and the remaining deadline/effective-operation provision at § 3163.

## Treatment of former §§ 3164–3174
The former provisions are retained as `[Repealed]` placeholders. Their historical source credits and editorial notes are preserved, while operative text is removed and TOC headings are synchronized. This avoids accidental revival, preserves legislative history, and accurately records the chapter-wide repeal.

## Plan
The plan replaces §§ 3161–3163 using complete XML fragments that preserve the Act’s enacted roman-numeral and nested labels rather than editorially renumbering them and replaces §§ 3164–3174 with dynamically generated repealed fragments based on the actual current XML. The installer calculates all hashes and performs a disposable scratch application before writing the approved files.
