# Public Law 2-5 — Judicial Efficiency Act

## Disposition
Resolved with no new current-Code operation.

## Review
Public Law 2-5 imposed short sentencing ceilings on specified Title 18 offenses, broad chapter groups, uncited Title 18 offenses, and aggregate sentences. Its provisions operated as an external sentencing overlay rather than as clean substitutions of the penalty clauses in each affected section.

Public Law 19-149, § 6, expressly repealed Public Law 2-5 in its entirety. Sections 7 and 8 of Public Law 19-149 then enacted a replacement sentencing scheme covering the same listed offenses, chapter groups, residual offenses, and aggregate-sentence limitation. The first-round plan already handled Public Law 19-149’s express amendment to 18 U.S.C. § 3156.

## Current treatment
Adding Public Law 2-5’s obsolete ceilings to current section text or notes would conflict with the later replacement scheme. No BAT operation is created. Any future project to publish the current general sentencing overlay should codify Public Law 19-149, not the superseded Public Law 2-5 limits.
