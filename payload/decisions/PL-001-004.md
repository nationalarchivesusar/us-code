# Public Law 1-4 — Retracting of Resignations Act

## Disposition
No executable Code plan. The source defect is real and cannot be cured without an editorial assumption.

## Review
The enacted PDF contains only sections 1 and 2. Section 2(c) directs that “Section 3(a)” be codified as 18 U.S.C. § 918, but no section 3 exists. Section 2(a) makes retraction of an immediately effective resignation unlawful; section 2(b) permits retraction of a future-effective resignation.

The most plausible explanation is that “Section 3(a)” was intended to read “Section 2(a).” That is not certain enough for an automated positive-law insertion, particularly because the Act supplies no penalty and the archive records the Act as repealed without identifying a reliable repealing instrument.

## Current treatment
No § 918 is created. No statutory text is invented. The draft plan remains non-executable. A later authenticated correction, engrossed version, or express congressional direction could support a plan.
