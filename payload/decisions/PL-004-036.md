# Public Law 4-36 — Defense Heraldic Services Act of 2022

## Disposition
Approved for BAT application after automatic current-hash generation and scratch testing.

## Enacted effect
Section II repeals 10 U.S.C. § 7594 and reserves the number. Section III expressly creates 10 U.S.C. § 206, transferring heraldic-services authority from the Secretary of the Army to the Secretary of Defense.

## Structural resolution
The Act says to add § 206 at the end of subchapter II of chapter 48. Current positive-law Title 10 has no chapter 48 at that location; sections 201 through 205 occupy subchapter II of chapter 8. The section number, subchapter designation, subject matter, and instruction to add the provision “at the end thereof” make the intended placement clear. The chapter number is treated as an obsolete or clerically erroneous structural reference, not as authority to invent a different section number.

## Plan
The plan preserves the enacted wording—including the Act’s original punctuation and references—and:

1. reserves § 7594 and synchronizes its TOC heading;
2. inserts new § 206 immediately after § 205 in the existing subchapter II sequence; and
3. inserts the § 206 TOC item after § 205.

The installer calculates hashes from the user’s actual current XML and scratch-applies the plan before installation.
